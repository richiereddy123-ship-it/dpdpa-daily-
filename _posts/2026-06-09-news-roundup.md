## India: DPDPA Rules & Implementation

Union Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw highlighted that the recently notified Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025, aim to strike a balance between fostering innovation and safeguarding citizens' digital rights. Various analyses and workshops, including one organized by UT Chandigarh, are actively dissecting these rules, emphasizing their practical implications and India's unique "middle path" in data protection. Businesses are facing increasing pressure to achieve DPDPA compliance, with experts and service providers like SaralPrivacy and Scalefusion urging enterprises to secure data at the source and conduct readiness assessments to identify privacy gaps before enforcement tightens. The DPDPA introduces India's first comprehensive data protection framework, fundamentally altering how data is collected and used.

## India: Data Protection Board

The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) has initiated the process of establishing the Data Protection Board of India (DPBI) by inviting applications for the positions of chairperson and members. The formation of this independent adjudicatory body is crucial for the effective enforcement of the DPDPA and will play a pivotal role in addressing data protection grievances and imposing penalties.

## India: DPDPA Constitutional Challenges

The Supreme Court of India has issued notices regarding petitions challenging the constitutionality of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025. This move signals that the apex court is set to hear arguments on various aspects of the law, potentially impacting its future interpretation and application. Legal experts are closely analyzing the grounds for these challenges, which question fundamental elements of the framework.

## India: Interpretational Nuances & AI

Concerns persist regarding potential interpretational uncertainties within the DPDPA, particularly due to the absence of a statutory definition for "harm," which could complicate privacy adjudication. Commentators also point to regulatory blind spots in the framework, suggesting areas that may require further clarity or amendment. The intersection of DPDPA and Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a growing focus, with businesses urged to assess their readiness for new AI-related data risks under the Act, even as a broader societal understanding of digital privacy remains a challenge for many.
