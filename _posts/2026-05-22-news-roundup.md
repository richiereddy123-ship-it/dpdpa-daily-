## India: DPDPA Implementation & Business Readiness

Indian enterprises are urged to prioritize data security at the source as the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) implementation timeline progresses. Businesses, particularly startups, face significant operational overhauls, requiring comprehensive strategies for consent management, data processing, and accountability frameworks. The market is responding with new solutions, such as automated consent management platforms, designed to help organizations navigate these complex compliance requirements.

The DPDPA introduces significant changes for cross-border data transfers, moving from a whitelisting approach to a more nuanced framework that data fiduciaries must understand. Additionally, the Act mandates stringent data breach notification requirements, compelling organizations to report incidents promptly and transparently, underscoring the need for robust incident response plans. The intersection of DPDPA with artificial intelligence (AI) also poses new challenges, requiring careful consideration of data processing principles in AI model development and deployment.
