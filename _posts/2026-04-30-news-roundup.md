## India: DPDPA Enters Force with Finalized Rules

India's landmark Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023 has officially come into full effect this month, following the finalization and notification of the comprehensive DPDP Rules 2025. This significant development marks a new era for data privacy in the country, establishing a robust framework for processing personal data and introducing new obligations for data fiduciaries and rights for data principals. Businesses across various sectors, from startups to banking and adtech, are now actively navigating the operational impacts and compliance requirements of the new legislation.

## India: Supreme Court Addresses DPDPA Challenges

The Supreme Court of India has issued notices to the government regarding multiple constitutional challenges filed against the Digital Personal Data Protection Act and its accompanying 2025 Rules. Petitions question various provisions of the Act, including concerns that certain clauses may dilute the Right to Information Act and potentially infringe upon fundamental rights. This signals ongoing legal scrutiny of the newly implemented privacy framework.

## India: Sectoral Compliance & Operational Impacts

With the DPDPA now live, industries are grappling with the practical implications of its provisions, with many seeking guidance on compliance. Reports indicate that startups are particularly concerned about the new compliance burden, while sectors like banking and adtech are analyzing specific operational impacts related to data processing, consent mechanisms, and cross-border data transfers. The Data Protection Board of India, once constituted, will play a crucial role in enforcement and interpretation.
