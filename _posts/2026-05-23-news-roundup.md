## India: DPDPA Takes Effect & Faces Scrutiny

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023, along with its associated Rules 2025, has officially entered into force, establishing a comprehensive legal framework for data protection across the country. This significant development introduces a uniform standard for processing personal data, impacting businesses and individuals nationwide. ([Source](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiekFVX3lxTFA0MUhxR2JRbkZEUG81Smp1Uy02Z0xtdWZYSnlyLVJVSHQ2eWtleENVY2Iya2tVTUNveFE5Mm1FLUVwd01icDNwT0pqcFZyOGJVNmZSRGJoSjJUT21BOWxkOXFqTmhVRmNSVW5jSGFyQkVlblRoR2FTX01R?oc=5))

The Supreme Court of India has issued notice on several petitions challenging the constitutionality of both the DPDPA 2023 and its Rules 2025. This legal scrutiny will be crucial in shaping the Act's long-term implementation and could introduce significant judicial interpretation to the new data protection regime. Legal commentators are highlighting potential interpretational ambiguities within the DPDPA, particularly concerning the absence of a statutory definition for "harm." This lack of explicit guidance could lead to challenges in privacy adjudication and is seen by some as a regulatory gap that may require future clarification. ## India: Compliance & Operational Impacts

Indian enterprises are being strongly advised to secure data at its origin and to thoroughly understand the DPDPA's operational implications, which extend to critical areas like managing data breaches and facilitating cross-border data transfers. Various compliance workshops are actively being launched to support businesses in adapting to these new requirements.
