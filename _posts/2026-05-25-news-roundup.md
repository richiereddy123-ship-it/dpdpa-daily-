## India: DPDPA Takes Effect

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023 has officially come into effect with the finalization and notification of the accompanying DPDP Rules 2025. This landmark legislation introduces a unified data protection framework across India for the first time, establishing new obligations for data fiduciaries and rights for data principals. The comprehensive rules provide essential guidance for implementation, covering various aspects of data processing and compliance.

## India: Compliance & Operational Focus

Indian enterprises are now actively reviewing their data handling practices to ensure DPDPA compliance, with a strong emphasis on securing data at the source. Key operational impacts include updated requirements for data breach reporting, new mechanisms for cross-border data transfers, and considerations for how the Act intersects with emerging technologies like AI. The banking sector, in particular, is undertaking detailed assessments to align its extensive data processing activities with the new regulatory landscape.

## India: Legal Challenges & Interpretations

The Supreme Court has issued notice on several petitions challenging the constitutional validity of the DPDPA and its recently notified rules, signaling potential legal battles ahead. Legal experts are also highlighting interpretational uncertainties within the Act, particularly concerning the absence of a statutory definition for "harm" and other potential regulatory blind spots that may require clarification through future adjudication or amendments.
